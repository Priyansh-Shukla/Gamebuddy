Ashina Reservoir Revisited is the late-game return to the area of the prologue. After defeating the Divine Dragon and returning from Fountainhead Palace, the player is summoned back to Ashina Castle by a series of scripted Sculptor's Idol dialogue events; the castle has now been seized by the Interior Ministry, the sky is on fire, and most of the idols across previously-explored regions are grayed out. The Reservoir is one of the few areas that becomes freshly traversable in this state.

The area is restructured for the late game. The well that begins the prologue is still climbable, but the surrounding rooftops are now patrolled by Ministry shinobi and elite Lone Shadow swordsmen rather than the Ashina samurai that originally held the position. The water level of the reservoir has dropped, exposing a new underwater path through a sluice grate that leads to a previously unreachable underground passage beneath the castle.

The Reservoir's mini-boss roster — including Seven Ashina Spears: Shikibu Toshikatsu Yamauchi — and a handful of optional Lone Shadow encounters provide some of the cleanest late-game Posture-fight reps available before the final boss sequence. Several Prayer Beads, Gourd Seeds, and Spirit Emblems are also locked behind the late-game state of the area, making it a near-mandatory detour for any player optimizing their final-boss stats.

Trivia: the choice to make the prologue's first area also serve as the late-game's last navigable hub is one of the cleanest examples of Sekiro's "everywhere is connected" world design philosophy, and the lighting shift between the moonlit prologue and the firelit late-game version of the same rooftops is one of the most consistently noted visual storytelling beats.
