The Demon of Hatred is the optional late-game super-boss fought in a burning courtyard at the rear of Ashina Outskirts, accessed by approaching a specific Sculptor's Idol after the Great Shinobi Owl has been defeated and the late-game Ministry siege has ignited the castle. He is enormous, three-armed, wreathed in actual flame, and his entire moveset is built on fire-attribute claw sweeps, ground-shattering slams, and oversized aerial leaps.

The fight is structurally a Soulsborne-style giant-boss encounter dropped into a Sekiro context. The Demon's Posture meter is functionally irrelevant — he cannot be Posture-broken into Deathblows the way humanoid bosses can. Instead, the fight is won by chipping his Vitality across three distinct phases, each with a new perilous attack added to the rotation: an arena-spanning charge in phase one, a long-range flame-thrower in phase two, and a horn-stab-plus-explosion combo in phase three. The Mist Raven prosthetic, Firecrackers, and the Malcontent's Ring all see heavy use here.

Defeating the Demon of Hatred yields a Memory, the Lord's Black Mortal Blade upgrade material, and one of the largest single Attack Power increases in the game. He is not on the critical path — players can complete all four endings without ever facing him — but is widely considered the most mechanically distinct boss in the game.

Trivia: the Demon of Hatred is canonically a transformed character whose identity is revealed in the dialogue surrounding the fight and in the Divine Confetti-laden item descriptions of his arena, and the in-universe explanation of his existence is the result of the Sculptor's long-suppressed Shura aspect finally manifesting.
