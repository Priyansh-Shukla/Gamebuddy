The Guardian Ape is the major boss of Sunken Valley, fought in a wide circular pit at the base of the Gun Fort. He is a huge, scarred white ape with chained iron rings on his wrists and a broken sword embedded in his neck. The first phase plays as an oversized animal boss: wide-sweep claw combos, a perilous-grab tell from a hunched posture, throwing of his own feces as a Terror-inflicting projectile, and several leap-slam attacks that span the arena.

After the first phase appears to end with a Deathblow that cleanly decapitates him, the fight pivots into one of the game's signature mechanical surprises: the ape gets back up, picks up the sword from his now-headless neck, and fights phase two as a posture-based swordsman with a one-armed katana stance and an entirely new attack pool. Phase two introduces a Terror-inflicting scream the ape unleashes by raising its severed head — Pacifying Agent is essentially mandatory or the Terror meter fills inside ten seconds — and a pair of long horizontal slashes that demand deflection rather than dodge.

Defeating both phases yields a Memory (Attack Power), the Lapis Lazuli rare upgrade material that unlocks the highest tier of Prosthetic Tool upgrades, and a Sculptor's Idol shortcut back to the upper valley. The ape is also the in-universe origin of one of the late-game Headless variants encountered in the Ashina Depths region.

Trivia: the resurrection-after-decapitation twist is one of the most discussed moments of the game and is the explicit dramatic high point of the Sunken Valley arc. The white ape silhouette references a Japanese folkloric class of mountain spirit (hihi) associated with abducting villagers, an idea the area's NPC dialogue alludes to repeatedly.
