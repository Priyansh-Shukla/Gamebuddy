The Shichimen Warrior is one of the apparition-type mini-bosses of Sekiro, a robed and seven-faced specter that floats above the ground and attacks primarily with crackling purple beams of malice. The first one is encountered in a flooded chamber within the Abandoned Dungeon, though several more appear elsewhere in the world later.

The fight follows the same preparation rule as the Headless: Divine Confetti is effectively required to deal real damage, and Pacifying Agent or Dousing Powder mitigates the Terror buildup that the Shichimen's attacks inflict. Mechanically the encounter is the opposite of the Headless's slow-motion grapple: the Shichimen rains long-range purple projectiles, summons swarms of homing skull-orbs, and floats out of melee range, forcing the player to chase, jump, and weave. Closing the gap and chaining sword pressure under Confetti is the fastest path to victory.

Defeating a Shichimen yields a Yashariku's Sugar (a powerful temporary damage-up consumable) or related items, and the encounter pattern is reused often enough that mastering one Shichimen meaningfully improves later runs.

Trivia: the warrior's seven-faced mask references the Shichimen Bodhisattva tradition in some schools of Japanese Buddhism, and the purple-flame imagery is consistent with the game's broader visual language for apparitions and Mibu-affiliated supernatural threats.
