The Headless Ape encounter is the return fight to the Guardian Ape, staged in a poisoned clearing in Ashina Depths. The original Guardian Ape — now permanently headless — reappears here using the phase-two sword moveset from the original fight, joined partway through by a second, smaller, brown ape companion that doubles the aggression and adds new flanking pressure.

Mechanically the fight is a stamina test built on top of skills the player has already developed. The headless ape's individual moveset is the phase-two pattern of the Guardian Ape, so deflection timing is already familiar, but the second ape's presence breaks any clean dueling rhythm — she throws unblockable claw swipes from off-angle and revives the headless ape if he is downed without sufficient Posture damage to chain a real Deathblow. Killing the brown ape first is the standard strategy and significantly shortens the fight.

Defeating both apes yields a Memory and a Prayer Bead. The fight is the canonical end of the Sunken Valley / Depths regional arc and is one of the cleanest "callback" boss encounters in the game.

Trivia: the brown ape in this encounter is canonically the Guardian Ape's mate, referenced obliquely in the original fight's environmental dressing (the apple offerings, the central altar), and the encounter is the in-universe explanation for why the original Ape was so attached to the Lapis Lazuli relic.
