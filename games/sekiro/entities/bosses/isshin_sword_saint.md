Isshin, the Sword Saint is the final boss of all three non-Shura endings, fought in a wide grass field on a clifftop near the prologue's tower at Ashina Reservoir. The fight is widely regarded as the apex of the game's combat system and is the encounter most often cited in discussions of Sekiro as a competitive duel.

The fight has four phases. Phase one opens against Genichiro Ashina, fought one last time in his Way of Tomoe stance — a structural callback to the player's first major encounter at Ashina Castle and a final test of lightning-reversal timing. Phase two transitions cleanly into Isshin's swordplay: tight katana combos, perilous thrusts demanding fast Mikiri, and an arsenal of stance-shifts that read almost like a compressed greatest-hits of every duelist in the game. Phase three adds a matchlock rifle to Isshin's offhand, layering ranged pressure on top of the existing swordplay and demanding the Loaded Umbrella for sustained survival. Phase four adds spear-thrusts and a lightning-summon mechanic — Isshin calls down lightning the way the Divine Dragon did, and the player must reverse the bolt to break his rhythm.

Defeating Isshin triggers the final-boss prompt where the player offers items to Kuro: only Divine Dragon's Tears → Immortal Severance ending; Divine Dragon's Tears + Everblossom → Purification ending; Divine Dragon's Tears + Frozen Tears → Return ending. The choice is made at runtime at the prompt and is one of the few cases in the game where the ending is not committed until the final cutscene begins.

Trivia: Isshin in his prime is the in-universe founder of the Ashina Arts skill tree, and the fight is the canonical demonstration of every technique the player has learned across the run being deployed back at them by a single master.
