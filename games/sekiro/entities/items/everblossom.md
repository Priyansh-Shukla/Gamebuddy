The Everblossom — also called the Aromatic Flower — is a key item received from Owl (Father) after defeating him in Hirata Estate Revisited. It is a small branch with permanently blooming white petals, and it is the structural unlock for the Purification ending of the game.

Mechanically the Everblossom is held in the inventory as a key item and is consumed only at the final-boss sequence: at the very end of the Isshin, the Sword Saint encounter, the player is given a contextual prompt to offer the Everblossom to Kuro alongside the Divine Dragon's Tears. Offering both triggers the Purification ending cutscene; offering only the Divine Dragon's Tears triggers the Immortal Severance ending instead.

The Everblossom is not consumable in any other context, and there is no other in-game use for it. Acquiring it is the entire point of the Purification side-quest chain, and possessing it gives the player the runtime choice of which ending to pursue at the final-boss prompt.

Trivia: the Everblossom is one of the few items in the game whose visual design — a perpetually blooming branch — is drawn from a specific class of Buddhist iconography (the udumbara, a flower said to bloom only once in three thousand years), and its inclusion is one of several callbacks the Purification ending makes to Senpou Temple's broader visual language.
