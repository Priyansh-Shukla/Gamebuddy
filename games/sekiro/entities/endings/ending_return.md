The Return ending is triggered by progressing the Dragon's Homecoming side-quest chain — speaking to the Divine Child of Rejuvenation in the Senpou Temple Inner Sanctum, repeatedly asking her for rice on consecutive rest cycles until she sleeps, delivering Rice for Kuro to Ashina Castle, acquiring the Holy Chapter: Dragon's Return from the old woman near Ashina Reservoir, collecting both Fresh and Dried Serpent Viscera, returning to the Divine Child for the Frozen Tears, and offering both Frozen Tears and Divine Dragon's Tears to Kuro at the final-boss prompt after defeating Isshin, the Sword Saint.

The ending cutscene depicts the Divine Child of Rejuvenation absorbing the Dragon's Heritage from Kuro into herself, completing her role as the vessel meant to return the Heritage to its western origin. Wolf and the Divine Child then travel west across snowbound mountains in the closing montage, framed as an actual journey rather than a metaphor — the Heritage is being physically returned to the homeland of the Divine Dragon. The journey is explicitly framed as an open-ended continuation rather than a final resolution.

This is widely considered the "true" or most complete ending of the four. It is the only ending that addresses the in-universe root cause — the displacement of the Dragon's Heritage from its western origin — rather than treating the symptoms within Ashina. The Dragon's Homecoming quest chain is also the most fragile of the side branches to execute, with several time-sensitive interactions that can be missed if the player progresses past their trigger points.

Trivia: the Divine Child's western journey is foreshadowed in the Divine Dragon's grove cutscene at Fountainhead Palace, and the cherry blossoms that follow the dragon to its grove are the in-universe physical evidence that the Dragon's Heritage originated in a western land — an idea the Sekiro Shadows Die Twice expansion materials and director commentary have confirmed.
