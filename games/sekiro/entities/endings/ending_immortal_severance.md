The Immortal Severance ending is the default neutral resolution of the non-Shura branch. It is triggered by progressing the late game normally — defeating the Great Shinobi Owl, defeating Isshin, the Sword Saint at Ashina Reservoir, and offering only the Divine Dragon's Tears to Kuro at the final-boss prompt without also offering the Everblossom or the Frozen Tears.

The ending cutscene depicts Wolf performing the Immortal Severance ritual on Kuro with the Mortal Blade — severing the Dragon's Heritage bond that has kept Kuro alive against death. Kuro dies in Wolf's arms, the Heritage is destroyed rather than transferred, and the closing frames depict Wolf returning to the Dilapidated Temple as the new Sculptor, carving Buddha statues in the workshop the original Sculptor maintained.

This is the "loyal but resigned" ending of the game: Wolf keeps his oath to Kuro by giving him the end Kuro asked for, but does so by performing the act of ending him rather than saving him. It is the canonical baseline ending from which the Purification and Return endings deviate, and it is the cleanest of the four to obtain — no side-quest chains are required, only the main critical path.

Trivia: the closing scene of Wolf as the new Sculptor is one of the game's clearest narrative completions of the symbolic ring established at the Dilapidated Temple in the prologue, in which the original Sculptor's presence and dialogue have been foreshadowing this particular resolution since the opening hours of the run.
