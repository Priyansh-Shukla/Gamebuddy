The Shura ending is triggered by the player choosing to obey the Iron Code and forsake Kuro during the rooftop confrontation with the Great Shinobi Owl at Ashina Castle. The choice immediately commits the run to a shortened final act that bypasses Fountainhead Palace, the Aromatic Flower chain, the Dragon's Homecoming chain, and Isshin, the Sword Saint entirely.

The branch consists of two scripted boss encounters fought back-to-back in the castle keep: Emma, the Gentle Blade, who attempts to stop Wolf from descending the tower, and Isshin Ashina (in his elderly, non-prime form) who arrives to finish the duel after Emma falls. The Isshin fight on this branch is mechanically distinct from the Sword Saint final boss — it is a single-phase katana duel with much tighter timings than Isshin's later prime form but without the Genichiro lead-in, the rifle phase, or the spear-and-lightning phase.

The ending cutscene depicts Wolf consumed by the Shura demon and slaughtering allies in a long pan across the burning castle, with closing narration confirming that Ashina province falls to flame and bloodshed in the aftermath. The Shura ending is widely considered the canonical "bad" ending of the four and is the only branch that cannot be combined with any of the other three on the same playthrough — once the Iron Code is followed, the run is committed.

Save-scumming around the Iron Code choice is the established community-recommended method of seeing the Shura ending on the same save as one of the other three, since the choice point can be reloaded if the player has a manual save backup from immediately before the rooftop dialogue.
