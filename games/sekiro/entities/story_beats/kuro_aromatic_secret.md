After defeating the Great Shinobi Owl, the Purification-ending branch opens through a chain of NPC dialogue events centered on Emma, Kuro, and the Sculptor. The chain begins by exhausting Emma's dialogue at the Dilapidated Temple; she eventually mentions that she has found a way to perform the Immortal Severance ritual without requiring Kuro's death and asks Wolf for help. The player must then travel to the Old Grave Sculptor's Idol, run through the gated archway to the two graves, and speak to Emma in that location, which extends the conversation further.

The next step is to return to the Dilapidated Temple and peek through a window behind the Sculptor's workshop to eavesdrop on a conversation between Emma and the Sculptor. The conversation reveals that the Sculptor was once a shinobi who himself attempted the same Immortal Severance ritual and partially succeeded, and that he has been suppressing a Shura aspect for most of his life. Speaking to Emma afterward yields a new bell — Father's Bell Charm — that can be offered at the Buddha to revisit the Hirata Estate memory under altered conditions.

This story beat is the unlock for the Hirata Estate Revisited memory. It is also the in-universe explanation of why the Sculptor maintains the Dilapidated Temple, why he has been so consistently present in Wolf's questline, and why his dialogue throughout the game contains repeated references to "another shinobi who tried this before."
