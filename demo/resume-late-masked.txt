WHERE YOU ARE
Standing on the rooftop of a burning Ashina Castle, face to face with the Great Shinobi Owl. The Divine Dragon's Tears are in hand from Fountainhead Palace, and the Owl has just delivered the Iron Code ultimatum: forsake Kuro and obey your father, or break the code and stand against him.

HOW YOU GOT HERE
After cracking the structural skill check of Genichiro Ashina on the castle keep — which unlocked the Mushin Arts tree and opened Senpou, Sunken Valley, and Ashina Depths simultaneously — you worked the mid-game in order. Senpou gave up the Armored Warrior and the Folding Screen Monkeys, putting the Mortal Blade in your inventory. Sunken Valley fell next (Snake Eyes Shirahagi and Long-arm Centipede Giraffe), then the Depths and Mibu Village, where you beat the Corrupted Monk on the misted bridge. With Mortal Blade plus Monk down, the gate to Fountainhead Palace opened — you climbed the Okami court, dropped the True Corrupted Monk on the cherry-blossom bridge, and ended the realm by stabbing lightning into the Divine Dragon for the Tears.

WHY YOU'RE DOING THIS
You're Wolf, a shinobi sworn to Kuro the Divine Heir, whose Dragon's Heritage is the prize the Ashina clan and the Interior Ministry have been tearing the country apart over. Genichiro took Kuro and your arm in the prologue; the Sculptor fit you the Shinobi Prosthetic at the Dilapidated Temple and Resurrection has carried you since. The Divine Dragon's Tears are the central key item every ending pivots on — which is exactly why the Owl, presumed dead since the Hirata raid, has surfaced on this rooftop to claim them and Kuro for himself. The dialogue prompt in front of you is the lock-in point of the entire run.
